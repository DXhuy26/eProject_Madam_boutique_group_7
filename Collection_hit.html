Chanel

For Virginie Viard’s first Métiers D'Art show (and her second for the house overall) the creative director kept the show local at the Grand Palais instead of venturing out to a far flung destination. She is after-all still establishing what her Chanel is—and Chanel is Paris, always. It’s also little boucle suits and that famed double C logo, two of the house’s hallmarks she leaned into for the pre-collection. The set was modeled after 31 rue Cambon in Paris, Gabrielle Chanel’s famed apartment as reimagined by director and former Chanel intern Sofia Coppola. "There is a sort of simplicity in going back to Chanel’s ABCs,” Virginie Viard explains via press release. “We don’t need to do too much. I didn’t want the usual long-distance traveling of the Métiers d’art collections; I wanted to stay in Paris. So, we had to think of a new way of doing things." Because, after all, Chanel est Chanel est Chanel.
